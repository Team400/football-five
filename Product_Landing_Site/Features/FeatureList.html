Key Features

Dashboard

Description
As a user of FantasyMax, I want the ability to create a division with a name of my choosing. This division will be exclusively comprised of my group's teams and the app will provide a dashboard that shows the current performance statistics and ranking of the teams in my division. These statistics will be updated within the hour following any regular season game in which any of my division's teams competes. Team names will be chosen by the team managers limited by a system to ensure no duplication of names within the division.


Constraints
There will be a limit on the maximum number of teams in a division to avoid crowding of the dashboard and to limit administrative overhead. There will be a system for monitoring division and team names to ensure that they are legal, unique, and not offensive.


Comments
To avoid problems with division and team names, it may be necessary to require that users choose their names from a pre-approved list rather than creating their own. This would have the added benefit of eliminating duplicated because when a stock name was chosen, it would be removed from the list so that it could not be chosen again.

News Feed

Description
As a user of FantasyMax, I want the dashboard to include a customizable news feed that can be filtered for news pertaining to one of the teams in my division or the players on those teams. The news feed will allow a user to pay for a premium feed that may be of higher quality and provide more in-depth coverage than the standard free feeds.


Constraints
Administering premium feeds would add a layer of complexity to the app and would require interfacing with the providers of those feeds. Thus, it may or may not be feasible to provide this level of service to the app's users, pending further investigations.


Comments
This would require calls to one or more API's but API calls have become a mainstay of modern web/mobile applications.



Pre-season Compendium

Description
As a college football novice, I want FantasyMax to provide a pre-season compendium of information about the games, teams and players to assist me during the draft. This compendium will be kept current regarding any last-minute developments. After the draft, the compendium will transition to an ongoing season commentary on the actual NCAA teams and players as well as fantasy divisions and players. Division-specific content will be submitted by division representatives and will be published pending editorial review.


Constraints
While not technically challenging, the quality of the compendium (and whatever it will be called after the draft) is a function of the knowledge and expertise of its authors and the time and effort they can manage to devote to the project.

Team Spreadsheet

Description
As a user of FantasyMax, I want an associated web application that includes a user modifiable spreadsheet. I will use it to rank my wish list of players during the draft and when a player is assigned to me, he will be automatically added to my team roster on a second page of the spreadsheet, where I can view and monitor my players’ statistics as the season progresses. The team roster will have a set of columns for pre-defined statistics, not modifiable by me, but there will also be a set of columns where I can include data of my own choosing for each player.


Constraints
Space on the spreadsheet will be limited to prevent storage overload. This includes a limit on the number of “free-text” columns on the team roster.


Comments
This will appeal to users of a specific orientation. Usage of the spreadsheet will be monitored to determine its relative value in the overall feature set.
